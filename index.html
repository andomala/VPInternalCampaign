Strengthening Connections to Industry

Geomatics has historically had a really high employment rate, however some of the second years have voiced concerns about jobs and simply not knowing what path they want to take. As VP Internal, I’ll work hand-in-hand with VP External to bring more networking nights on different days that accomodate well with students schedules. Ensuring these events are held on various days accomodates weekly commitments so that more people can attend the event.

I’ll also work VP External to organize GESS-led mock interviews and resume roasts to help you shine when it matters most. But it doesn’t stop there. I know how crucial co-ops and internships are, and I’ll work with faculty to ensure earlier job postings and deadline notifications, so you never miss out on an opportunity to apply!

Plus, I’ll work VP Academic to restructure the peer mentorship program to connect you with upper-years who’ve already navigated the co-op process successfully and have landed a job. I’ll make sure you’re not just ready for the industry — you’re ahead of the engineering curve and fully informed!

Academic Support and Resource Development

I’m on a mission to make sure no current or potential Geomatics student ever feels lost or unsupported. Prior to major selection, I saw firsthand how many of my peers had no idea what Geomatics was—and how much that lack of understanding held them back. Even to the people who chose Geomatics, they ended up having a misinformed idea of what the program would be like.

That’s why, as VP Internal, I’ll work with VP Academic to create introductory sessions for first-year students, where they can experience what Geomatics classes are like and get a taste of the incredible opportunities this field offers. These sessions will be a massive boon for students who are still figuring out their path.

I also know how tough courses like MATH 375 and ENDG 319 can be, especially this year was brutal and it doesn't seem to be easing up next year! That’s why I’ll establish a peer tutoring network to give students the support they need to succeed. No one should feel like they’re struggling alone, and with this network, you’ll always have someone to turn to.

And let’s talk about a common issue which is a lack of information and sometimes even misinformation. Too many students in my year were given wrong advice about course requirements, minors, and more. That ends now. I’ll create a comprehensive FAQ for course information, clearing up confusion and giving you the accurate, reliable answers you need. I'll work with the VP Communications and the Artistic Commissioners to best broadcast this info. Whether it’s about minors, course selection, questions relating to CBEPS, or anything in between, I’ll make sure you have the resources to make informed decisions about your academic journey!
